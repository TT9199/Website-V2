British values 

British values focus on democracy, the rule of law, individual liberty, mutual respect, and tolerance of different faiths and beliefs. Together, they form a civic framework that guides how people live, work, and participate in public life across the UK. These principles emphasize fair decision‑making, equal rights under the law, freedom to make personal choices, and respect for the diverse communities that make up modern Britain.
